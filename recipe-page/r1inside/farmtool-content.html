Tools used in farming

Shovel

A shovel is a tool used for digging and moving soil, sand, gravel, snow, and other materials. It typically consists of a long handle made of wood, metal, or plastic, and a scoop or blade made of metal. The blade is usually flat and pointed, and can be either straight or curved, depending on the intended use.

Shovels come in different shapes and sizes, depending on their intended use. For example, a square-point shovel is good for digging and moving loose materials, such as soil and gravel, while a round-point shovel is better for digging and moving compacted materials, such as clay and sod. A trenching shovel has a narrow blade that is good for digging trenches, while a scoop shovel has a large, deep blade that is good for moving large amounts of loose materials, such as snow.

plant cutter

A plant cutter is a tool used for trimming or pruning plants, such as trees, bushes, hedges, and flowers. It is typically made of metal, with sharp blades that can be straight or curved, and two handles that are used to operate the cutting mechanism. Plant cutters come in different shapes and sizes, depending on their intended use. For example, a hand pruner is a small plant cutter that is used for trimming small branches and stems, while a lopper is a larger plant cutter that is used for cutting thicker branches and stems. A hedge shear is a plant cutter that is used for trimming hedges and bushes, while a pruning saw is a plant cutter that is used for cutting larger branches and stems.

sickle

A sickle is a handheld agricultural tool with a curved blade that is used for harvesting crops, such as grains, vegetables, and grasses. It is typically made of metal, with a short handle made of wood or plastic that is attached to the blade. The blade of a sickle is curved to allow for efficient cutting of crops in a sweeping motion. The sharp edge of the blade is used to slice through the stems of the plants, while the curvature of the blade helps to gather the cut plants together for easy collection and transportation.

Axe

An axe is a tool that consists of a blade made of metal that is attached to a handle made of wood or composite material. Axes are used for a variety of tasks, such as chopping wood, splitting logs, and felling trees. The blade of an axe is typically wedge-shaped and is designed to cut through wood fibers with a chopping motion. The blade can be single-edged or double-edged, and the handle can be straight or curved, depending on the intended use.

Chain saw

A chainsaw is a handheld power tool that consists of a chain with a series of teeth that rotate around a guide bar. Chainsaws are used for a variety of tasks, such as cutting trees, trimming branches, and harvesting firewood. The chain of a chainsaw is driven by a gasoline engine, an electric motor, or a battery-powered motor. The teeth of the chain are designed to cut through wood fibers with a rapid, spinning motion. The guide bar provides support for the chain and helps to direct the cutting action.
